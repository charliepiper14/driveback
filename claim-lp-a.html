Your questions, answered

Everything you need to understand the motor finance redress scheme.

It's a way to seek compensation if your car finance was mis-sold. Many drivers paid more than they should have because dealers or brokers earned undisclosed commission. If that commission was never properly explained to you, you could be owed money back. Successful claims may return up to £829* per vehicle. Not all consumers will be eligible — see the FCA statement.
The FCA estimates an average of around £829* per eligible agreement under its motor finance compensation scheme, including simple interest under its methodology. With most drivers holding around two agreements, that works out at roughly £1,658 on average. Actual payouts may be higher or lower depending on your circumstances. There's no guarantee a representative recovers more than you could yourself.

Yes. If you financed multiple vehicles, you may be able to claim for each mis-sold agreement. Each one is assessed separately, and any compensation depends on the specifics of that agreement.

Following the FCA statement of 30 March 2026, the scheme runs in two periods (6 April 2007–31 March 2014 and 1 April 2014–1 November 2024). The scheme opens 30 June 2026 for later agreements and 31 August 2026 for earlier ones, with the final deadline to claim being 31 August 2027. Submitting earlier generally means an earlier decision — and you don't need to wait for your lender to contact you.
